Problem & mission

The demand is real. It's just invisible.

The problem

Rwanda's HSSP V and the "4x4" reform prioritize decentralizing mental health care, yet rural gaps persist: 11.9% of the population, and up to 35.6% of vulnerable groups, face major depressive disorders, but isolated data silos and deep-rooted stigma keep that demand hidden even where clinics exist. Regional centers like CARAES Ndera are digitized, but most local clinics still can't track mental health cases, and 58,000 community health workers are being asked to adopt mobile record systems with no low-burden tools to do it.

Our mission

To make stigma-free mental health support reachable from any phone, in any village. By pairing an anonymous, low-bandwidth access point with a growing network of private, community-based care spaces, we aim to give 80% of Rwanda's remote communities safe, immediate access to quality psychological support by 2035.

The solution

Dial in. Stay anonymous. Get routed to care.

An anonymous, zero-rated USSD-based mental health triage and referral system: accessible from any basic phone, with no internet or data cost. A short, private check-in sorts each caller into exactly the level of care they need.

Tier 1 · Crisis

Routed directly to the RBC Crisis Hotline.

Tier 2 · Referral

Referred to a partnered health center or community sociotherapy group.

Tier 3 · Self-help

Sent self-help information automatically via SMS.

Testing the solution

Try a real session

This is what a caller experiences on a basic, data-free phone. Dial *124#, choose Mental Health, and answer honestly: the same four questions a real caller would answer to be sorted into the right tier of care.

  • Dial *124#: then select Mental Health from the menu.
  • Answer 4 short questions: on mood, thoughts, daily function, and support.
  • Get routed instantly: to a crisis line, a referral, or self-help SMS.
  • Choose to opt in: for a callback, which is the only moment your number is ever attached to your session.
